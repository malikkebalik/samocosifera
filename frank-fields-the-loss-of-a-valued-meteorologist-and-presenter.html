Frank Fields: The Loss of a Valued Meteorologist and Presenter

Let’s take a look at the obituary of Frank Fields and the circumstances surrounding his death. Fields, a prominent figure in the field of meteorology, passed away at the age of 100. For half a century, he was a familiar face on American television, based in New York City, where he provided weather updates and valuable information on a range of scientific and health-related topics. Notably, he played a crucial role in promoting awareness about the Heimlich Maneuver.

Frank Fields’ Passing and Cause of Death

Dr. Frank Field, a trailblazing meteorologist who first brought weather forecasts to New York television news and later gained recognition as a commentator and presenter for various network science, space, and medical programs, passed away in Florida at the age of 100. The news of his death was reported by WNBC-TV in New York, where he began his broadcasting career in 1958. While the specific cause of Frank Field’s death has not been disclosed, it was attributed to natural causes.

Despite lacking a degree in meteorology, Field held a Ph.D. in optometry before transitioning to television. His military background as a weather forecaster led to his recognition as a meteorologist and the prestigious “Seal of Approval” from the American Meteorological Society. This esteemed accolade is reserved for individuals capable of delivering accurate and dependable weather information. Dr. Field’s legacy includes influencing two other notable weather forecasters, Storm Field and Allison Field, who enjoyed successful careers in New York television. He is survived by his children, Pamela Field, Storm Field, and Allison Field, as well as seven grandchildren and six great-grandchildren. Unfortunately, his wife, Joan Kaplan Field, passed away earlier this year.

Frank Fields’ Financial Assets and Career at WNBC

While Dr. Frank Field gained recognition from his work as a meteorologist and television personality, he maintained privacy regarding personal details, including his net worth. Nonetheless, numerous reports estimate his net worth to be around $5 million. Dr. Field had a lengthy and prosperous tenure in television, which served as his livelihood. He embarked on his broadcasting journey in 1958 at WNBC-TV in New York, where he initially focused on weather forecasts before expanding his role to encompass presenting and commentating on network science and health programs.

Dr. Field became a prominent figure, earning acclaim for his expertise and dedication during his 40-year tenure in New York and network television. Apart from meteorology and presenting, he also delved into other areas related to his profession. Utilizing his technical expertise, he analyzed data from weather satellites and conveyed weather phenomena to the public through visual television presentations. He additionally served as a science reporter, covering topics beyond weather, including live broadcasts of cardiac surgeries and organ transplants.

Dr. Field made significant contributions to public health and safety. He played a pivotal role in popularizing the Heimlich maneuver, a life-saving technique developed by Dr. Henry J. Heimlich. Field’s advocacy and demonstrations of the maneuver were instrumental in raising awareness and saving lives.
