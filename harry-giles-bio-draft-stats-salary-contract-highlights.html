Harry Giles Biography

Harry Giles born as Harry Lee Giles III is an American professional basketball player for the Sacramento Kings of the national basketball Association(NBA). He won the Jordan Brand Classic and Nike Hoop Summit awards in 2016.

He plays in the Power Forward/Center position and he was one of the top performers for Team USA at the U19 FIBA Americas Tournament in 2015.

Harry Giles Age

He was born on April 22, 1998, in Winston-Salem, North Carolina, U.S. He is 20 years old as of 2018.

Harry Giles Height

He is 2.08 m tall.

Harry Giles Family

He is the son of Harry Giles II and Melissa Addison Giles. His father, Harry Giles II, played college basketball and football at Winston-Salem State University. He has one older brother; Marquis Dalton and 3 older sisters; Shari Watson, Shalaria Taylor, and Quanetrea Taylor.

Harry Giles High School

He attended the Wesleyan Christian Academy in High Point, North Carolina. For the three years that he spent with them, he played with the school basketball team averaging 12.5 points and 9.5 rebounds per game in his freshman year. In that year, Wesleyan Christian High School won the 2013 NCISSA 3A State Championship. The next year, he suffered a left knee injury and had to sit out the entire year.

By the time he was returning to court in his junior year, his school was ranked as the No. 2team in the country by USA Today. Giles went hard chipping in with points scored and rebounds making his school record victories over other schools.

He decided to change schools and ended up in Oak Hill Academy in Mouth of Wilson, Virginia. He was disrupted by another knee injury and had to move to Forest Trail Academy in Kernersville, North Carolina. While at Forest Trail, he undertook online courses to help him complete his senior year.

After high school, he opted to commit to Duke University and made his first debut on December 19, 2016, in a win over Tennessee State. His first college career double-double came in a win over Georgia Tech where he scored 10 points and 12 rebounds. By the end of his freshman year, he had played a total of 26 games, averaging 3.9 points, 3.9 rebounds, and .4 assists.

Harry Giles Draft

Giles decided to forego his remaining three years of his college career and entered the 2017 NBA Draft. He got selected as the 20th overall pick by the Portland Trail Blazers but was later traded to the Sacramento Kings. On July 8, 2017, he signed his rookie contract but had an injury that sidelined him till the turn of the year.

In 2018, he was prevented from seeing the action but asked to focus and major on training and injury prevention. He later took part in the 2018 NBA Summer League with the Sacramento Kings and scored 17 points, 8 rebounds and 5 steals in a game against the Phoenix Suns. Besides all that, he was named to the All-Tournament Team as well.
