Cash Money Rapper B.G. Released from Prison After 11 Years

 Posted on August 31, 2024  |  1 minutes  |  157 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Cash Money Records artist, B.G. has been released from prison after serving 11 years.

B.G. was sentenced to 14 years in prison for firearm possession and obstruction of justice in 2012. Despite spending many birthdays behind bars, including his 42nd this past Sunday, he vowed that it would be his last in prison.

The 42-year-old rapper, whose real name is Christopher Dorsey, has been successfully transferred to community confinement, essentially a halfway house, and is projected to be fully released from custody on July 11, 2024.

The veteran rapper was captured on Birdman’s IG Live as he embraced him and other friends outside the federal prison in Herlong, California, where he had been for the past 11 years.

B.G. is a member of the original Hot Boys rap crew. He released several projects with Cash Money, most notably 1999’s “Chopper City In The Ghetto,” which coined the timeless jewelry term “Bling Bling” on the lead single.
