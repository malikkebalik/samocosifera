American professional wrestler Trevor Lee Caddell, better known by his ring name Cameron Grimes, is presently under contract with WWE. He gained notoriety most for his time as the NXT North American Champion. He began performing as Trevor Lee on the indie circuit in 2009, particularly in Pro Wrestling Guerilla (PWG), where he and Andrew Everett held the PWG Tag Team Championship.

After leaving Impact, he was given a job in NXT by WWE, where he later signed. On April 2, 2022, at NXT Stand & Deliver, he would capture the NXT North American Championship by defeating Carmelo Hayes, Santos Escobar, Solo Sikoa, and Grayson Waller in a fatal five-way ladder match. What is the Professional Career of Cameron Grimes?

Cameron Grimes begins his wrestling career by appearing for a number of independent promotions. On March 28, 2014, at “Mystery Vortex II,” he makes his debut with Pro Wrestling Guerrilla (PWG), losing to Andrew Everett in a three-way match that also features Cedric Alexander. On July 26, 2014, at “Eleven,” he defeated three-time PWG World Champion in a stunning shock victory. He was one of the 24 competitors in the 2014 Battle of Los Angeles event. On October 17, 2014, at “Untitled II,” he dethroned Adam Cole, the longest-reigning PWG World Champion in the title’s history. He then got his first shot at the PWG World Championship against Roderick Strong on February 27, 2015, at From Out of Nowhere, but he was unsuccessful.

On May 22, 2015, he and Andrew Everett defeated the PWG World Tag Team Champions The Beaver Boy to win the 2015 DDT4 competition. However, The Young Bucks defeated Lee and Everett for the PWG World Tag Team Championship on June 26, 2015, at “Mystery Vortex III.” At PWG’s All Star Weekend 11, Lee began to display villainous characteristics, including cheap shooting opponents prior to bouts and jeering the audience. Lee won on both nights, defeating Will Ospreay on night one and Matt Sydal on night two. He then competed at the 2017 Battle of Los Angeles, where Donovan Dijak knocked him out in the first round. He also lost to Rey Horus on night one and Morgan Webster on night two of PWG All Star Weekend 14.

More on career

He competed for other independent companies, such as OMEGA Championship Wrestling, where on May 21, 2015, he won the OMEGA Heavyweight Championship in a triple threat match that also featured his mentor Matt Hardy. On January 18, 2019, he lost a match at PWG’s Hand of Doom against the reigning champion Jeff Cobb. At All American Wrestling’s AAW Showdown 2018, he defeated DJZ to win the AAW Heritage Championship. In his first bout of 2019, he successfully defended his CWF Mid-Atlantic Heavyweight title against Cain Justice. the He made his Total Nonstop Action Wrestling (TNA) debut on the 12th August 2015 episode of “Impact Wrestling” and won the title against Tigre Uno in a two-rematch with Eddie Edwards and DJZ.

On the 6th July edition of “Impact,” he assaulted the newly crowned X Division Champion Sonjay Dutt after his bout with Caleb Konley and stole the title. On the 9th November episode of “Impact,” he would lose the title to Taiji Ishimori. After his contract with Impact Wrestling expired on December 31, 2018, he announced at a CWF Mid-Atlantic event on January 12, 2019, that he had signed a contract with WWE. He started making appearances at NXT Live events in the Florida region, and he defeated Shane Strickland in a dark match at Full Sail University prior to the first May “NXT” television tapings. On September 18 during NXT’s USA Network debut, he defeated Sean Maluta. He then challenged Keith Lee for the NXT North American Championship, which Lee accepted on February 26, 2020.

More on career

On the 12th August episode of “NXT,” he also defeated Velveteen Dream and Kushida to qualify for the NXT North American Championship ladder match at TakeOver XXX. On the 23rd September episode of “NXT,” he lost to Kyle O’Reilly in the first-ever Gauntlet Eliminator match to determine the #1 contender for the NXT Championship at TakeOver 31 against Finn Balor. After the match, Timothy Thatcher attacked him since Grimes had fiercely questioned him about being at ringside on December 9 of “NXT,” which led to Tommaso Ciampa’s victory.

He later made a comeback on NXT’s episode of February 10th, 2021, with a new gimmick, but he lost a gauntlet match at TakeOver: Stand & Deliver that was meant to set up a future North American Championship match. Additionally, he began a feud with Ted DiBiase of the WWE Hall of Fame after running into him in a jewelry store while shopping for watches on the April 27 “NXT” program. He competed against Carmelo Hayes for the NXT North American Championship on February 15, 2022, in the Vengeance Day special edition. Additionally, on April 2, 2022, at NXT Stand & Deliver, he would triumph over Carmelo Hayes, Santos Escobar, Solo Sikoa, and Grayson Waller to win the NXT North American Championship.

On the April 12 episode of “NXT 2.0,” he also made his first successful title defense against Solo Sikoa. In a Triple Threat match against Carmelo Hayes and Solo Sikoa at NXT Spring Breakin’, he would successfully make another title defense. He made a terrible mistake by losing the North American Championship to Hayes at NXT In Your House, ending his reign after only 63 days. He subsequently started a feud with NXT Champion Bron Breakker, which he ultimately lost to at NXT: The Great American Bash.
