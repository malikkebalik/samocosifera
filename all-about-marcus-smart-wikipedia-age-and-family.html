Marcus serves as the team’s point guard and shooting guard. He was originally chosen by the Celtics as the sixth overall choice in the 2014 NBA Draft. At Oklahoma State, he played basketball in college.

College career

Oklahoma State University became his preferred team. He led the Cowboys to a 24-8 record and third-place finish in the Big 12 during his first season. Marcus averaged 15.4 points, 5.8 rebounds, and 4.2 assists per game. He tied an OSU single-game scoring record in November 2013 when he scored 39 points against Memphis.

After a contentious altercation with Texas Tech in February 2014, Smart shoved a fan in the crowd and was suspended for three games. He said that the supporter used a racial slur against him. He was selected as one of the 30 finalists for the Naismith College Player of the Year award.

Marcus became the first player in NCAA tournament history to finish with 23 points, 13 rebounds, 7 assists, and 6 steals. He added 20 points, 10 rebounds, 5 assists, and 5 steals to his stat line. Marcus played 33.1 minutes per game while at Oklahoma State for two seasons, averaging 16.6 points, 5.9 rebounds, and 4.5 assists.
