Heidi Watney- Relationship

She tries not to reveal too much about her personal life to the public, but certain information about her husband is still available. Heidi and Mike Wickham met in 2012 while working on a project together; Mike is also a reporter. It took them a while to start dating, but they haven’t broken up since their first date. Mike proposed to Heidi in the summer of 2014, and they married on December 31, 2014, since they both enjoyed the concept of starting the new year as a married pair. They married in Edgewater, New Jersey, and spent their honeymoon on Nevis Island in the Caribbean, subsequently visiting Bora Bora.

Despite being married for many years, they decided to delay having children until Heidi gave birth to their son Jax on April 26, 2018. While Heidi hasn’t spoken about the other men she’s dated over the years, rumor has it that she was in a romantic connection with Jason Varitek, who worked with her at the Boston Red Sox. Jason was married when the accusations began, but it had no effect on his marriage because both he and Heidi officially denied the claims. However, everything changed when the paparazzi photographed Heidi and Jason in a club. Jason would eventually file for divorce, fueling the suspicions even further.

Heidi Watney- Professional Career

Heidi’s first professional choice was modeling. Heidi was discovered by modeling scouts, who asked her to a photo shoot, and she later signed with a modeling agency in Boston. Motivated by her newfound reputation in the modeling world, she attended the Miss California USA pageant, where she finished first runner-up despite not winning.

She began her career as a reporter in 2006 when she began working as a weekend sports anchor for KMPH-TV in Fresno — she also worked at ESPN Radio 1430 KFIG at the same time. In May 2008, she relocated across the nation to become a host of “The Ultimate Red Sox Show,” which airs on the NESN sports network, followed by becoming a host of “The Red Sox Report,” and finally working as an on-field reporter for the Boston Red Sox professional baseball team.

Heidi left NESN in November 2011 to work for Time Warner Cable SportsNet as a sideline reporter for all regional sports and relocated to Los Angeles because the job was situated there. She wasn’t with them for long, as she left in September 2012 and began working as the host of the Major League Baseball (MLB) “Quick Pitch” weeknights show in 2013 – she has been with them ever since only missing the start of 2018 to give birth to her son.

While she is not an actress, she appeared in Dropkick Murphys’ music video “Going Out in Style.”
