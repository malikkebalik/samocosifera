Francis Ngannou Career

Francis Ngannou Career: - Ngannou began his MMA career in November 2013 and has fought mostly for the French promotion 100% Fight as well as other regional promotions in Europe. Ngannou made his UFC debut on December 19, 2015 at UFC on Fox 17 against fellow newcomer Louis Henrik. He won the fight via knockout in the second round. Ngannou faced interim UFC Heavyweight Champion Cyril Gane at UFC 270 on 22 January 2022 in his first title defense. Three and a half weeks before the fight he injured knee ligaments. Ngannou won the fight via unanimous decision, his first career decision victory.

On January 14, 2023, Ngannou was stripped of the UFC Heavyweight Championship after he and the UFC could not come to terms on a new contract. Ngannou's contract expired in mid-December, and after the two parties could not reach an agreement, the UFC waived his one-year matching rights clause, making Ngannou an unrestricted free agent. The Francis Ngannou Foundation runs the first MMA gym in Cameroon, with the aim of providing space for young people to train.
